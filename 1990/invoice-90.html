About Maison Margiela’s runway presentations from 1989–1990 marked a radical rupture with the conventions of late-20th-century fashion. Under the direction of Martin Margiela, these early shows rejected spectacle, glamour, and elitism in favor of anonymity, deconstruction, and raw authenticity. Often staged in unconventional, marginalized spaces—such as playgrounds, abandoned buildings, or urban outskirts—the runways blurred the boundary between audience and environment, sometimes involving local residents and children rather than industry insiders. The garments themselves exposed their own construction: linings worn outward, seams left visible, and found materials repurposed into clothing, articulating a philosophy rooted in process over polish.
